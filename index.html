Prototypes

Neon Echowave

In this 2D cyberpunk-themed narrative, rhythm, driving game, I was challenged to work in 2D and effectively share my vision as a Product Owner and delegate work to my team of six, some of whom were in the office and others remote. My tasks also involved setting up meetings and, to a degree, acting as a Scrum Master to ensure that a product was presentable within the few weeks we had.

Besides the management role, I was the sole designer for the game. I aimed to create a laid-back narrative-focused driving experience that interacted with a dynamic synthwave soundtrack, featuring a branching karma system for replayability and mini-hacks to diversify the experience between the two protagonists.

Level & Environmental Design

I was introduced to Unity's Terrain Tools and ProBuilder for basic grey boxing to establish a scene.

I am drawn by the allure of adventure, which is why I always strive to provide that experience. With the mountain scene in particular, I aimed to create a slice of an open world featuring a few points of interest POI that enhance not just the vistas but also awaken curiosity in the player as they traverse the grand valley.

3D Graphics

Weight Painting was particularly intriguing and quite finicky, especially since I challenged myself to create facial expressions without using shape keys, aiming to convey a lot of character through its face.

I exclusively used Forward Kinematics FK due to my model's simple structure. Despite a tight deadline of one day per animation, I'm pleased with the outcomes. For smoother engine integration, I factored in animation blending, omitting some movements. For instance, I segmented the jump animation into three parts.

Pixel Art

Having never engaged with any type of art before, I was introduced to Pixel Art as an entry point. I found animation especially gratifying; once I got the pixels where I wanted them.

I started playing around with sub-pixels and was intrigued by just how advanced it could get.

Then, I proceeded to create a tilemap, setting up a scene with a few of my assets after experimenting with different styles.

About me

From a young age, video games have been a significant part of my life, a fascination ignited by my older brother. He introduced me to a new world, taking me on an unforgettable journey through the realms of Zelda, Tomb Raider, Warcraft, and Age of Mythology.

These adventures sparked not just an interest but a deep passion for video games that has only grown over time after exploring hundreds of titles. This led me to seek out an understanding of how I could assist in the creation of future games.

During my time at GameDev Indie, I dabbled in Programming, Pixel Art & 3D Graphics, Animation, VFX and Game Audio, Game Design, Project Management and UI & UX.

Today I'm excited to shape the future of gaming, embracing its evolving potential in entertainment.
